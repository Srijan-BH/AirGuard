Understanding Your AQI Value

Did you check another weather app or website and see a different AQI value? Here is why:

1. US EPA AQI Standard (0-500 Scale) This project implements the official US EPA Air Quality Index (0-500 scale). Other portals may use different standards (like the Indian CPCB index, UK DAQI, or European CAQI) which calculate completely different AQI values for the exact same pollutant concentrations.
2. Instantaneous vs. NowCast / 24-Hour Averages Our page fetches the **current hour's instantaneous** pollutant concentration from satellite data. Commercial platforms (like IQAir or AirNow) calculate AQI using NowCast or 24-hour averages, which smooth out hourly peaks and lag behind real-time spikes or drops.
3. Comprehensive Pollutant Coverage Our US EPA AQI calculation evaluates six key pollutants (PM2.5, PM10, CO, NO₂, SO₂, O₃) fetched in real-time, taking the worst-performing pollutant as the dominant driver. Other sites may only measure PM2.5, omitting gaseous spikes entirely.
